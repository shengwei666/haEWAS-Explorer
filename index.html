Why Heterogeneity-Adjusted EWAS (haEWAS)?

Epigenome-wide association studies (EWAS) have identified numerous DNA methylation alterations associated with disease and complex traits. To date, most EWAS rely on microarray-derived Beta values, which represent mean methylation levels in bulk tissues. However, the same mean methylation level can mask fundamentally different degrees of single-molecule methylation heterogeneity. While existing deconvolution methods can correct for cell-type abundance, they cannot resolve this intrinsic single-molecule variation. Historically, capturing this heterogeneity required whole-genome bisulfite sequencing (WGBS)—a method too costly for large population-based studies.

To bridge this gap, we developed a computational framework that infers methylation heterogeneity directly from standard methylation array signals. By establishing a mapping between mean methylation levels and single-molecule heterogeneity metrics—such as CHALM and CAMDA—our approach enables Heterogeneity-Adjusted EWAS (haEWAS) without the need for sequencing. This framework extends EWAS from a mean-driven association to one driven by inferred heterogeneity, empowering the re-analysis of massive existing array cohorts to uncover disease-associated epigenetic variation previously obscured by conventional EWAS.

This website hosts curated haEWAS meta-analysis summaries across diverse phenotypes, tissues, and analytical models, providing harmonized effect estimates under Beta, CHALM, and CAMDA metrics. Use the Explore tab for rich interactive filtering and sorting, or head to Download for reproducible CSV exports matching your selections.

Note: CHALM quantifies the promoter methylation as the ratio of methylated reads (with ≥1 mCpG) to total reads mapped to a given promoter region. CAMDA quantifies the concurrence of active DNA methylation and demethylation within the same cell by dissecting reads from bisulfite sequencing.
